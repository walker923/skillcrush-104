Sometimes the voices in your head are lying.

Hi. I'm Bob. I'm 61 years old, generally not an age when people take on learning new things, especially things that are technology-related.

Sometimes, the things I'm learning in Skillcrush seem overwhelming. The voices in my head start saying "You're too old for this," and "You're going to fail and make a fool of yourself."

I have an old manual typewriter that sits on top of a bookshelf to remind me of my roots in writing and journalism. I literally started out in communications before the age of computers -- personal computers, at least. Yes, I'm that old.

When I'm feeling lost in Skillcrush, I think that typewriter kind of looks at me and smirks.

I still don't know if I'll really be able to succeed in this new field. But I was able to build and publish a website using only HTML and CSS, and it actually looks pretty good. I know what I'm doing on this particular page, what the various HTLM tags and CSS instructions mean. When that happens, I think that maybe I'll make it after all. One day at a time.

Sometimes you have to ignore the voices in your head. Or tell them to shut the hell up.
